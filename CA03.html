Binary and Decimal Conversions (Advanced)

Conversions between the binary and decimal number systems are usually pretty straightforward. However, when numbers with decimals are introduced, the process for converting can be a little different.
Let's take a look at how this is accomplished.

Note: This explanation will assume you understand how to convert between basic binary and decimal. If you do not understand this, please go back and review the previous lesson.

Binary to Decimal 

-----------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------> Example 1

Convert 11011.10102 to decimal.
Just like when converting to decimal from binary, we first start by setting up our exponents. Make sure to start at the first place left of the decimal.

   1   1   0   1   1   .   1   0   1   0   
   --------------------------------------  
   24  23  22  21  20       2?  2?  2?  2?

We know that when we move left a column, we increase a power. But what happens when we move right?
Well, when we move right, we subtract the exponent by 1. So, since we start at 20 for the column directly left of the decimal, we subtract 1 from the exponent each time we move right. 

   1   1   0   1   1   .   1   0   1   0   
   --------------------------------------  
   24  23  22  21  20      2-1 2-2 2-3 2-4

Next, we evaluate our exponents. 

   1   1   0   1   1   .   1   0   1   0     
   --------------------------------------    
   16  8   4   2   1      .5  .25 .125 .0625

Finally, we just add our numbers where we see the number 1. In this case, we do 16 + 8 + 2 + 1 + .5 + .125
This totals to 27.625
So, 11011.10102 is 27.62510 in decimal. 

-----------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------> Example 2

Convert 0111.1102 to decimal.
So, we first start by setting up our exponents. 

   0   1   1   1   .   1   1   0   
   -----------------------------   
   23  22  21  20      2-1 2-2 2-3

Next, we evaluate the exponents. 

   0   1   1   1   .   1   1   0   
   -----------------------------   
   8   4   2   1       .5 .25 .125

Finally, we just add our exponents where we see the number 1. So, we do 4 + 2 + 1 + .5 + .25
This totals to 7.75
So, 0111.1102 is 7.7510 in decimal.

Decimal to Binary

Converting back from decimal to binary is a bit more difficult. In this case, we will use two different operations.
For the part of the number that is to the left of the decimal, we will do the remainder method from before.
For the part of the number to the right of the decimal, we will double it, and then put a 1 or a 0 depending on the result. 

-----------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------> Example 1

Convert 30.781252 to binary.
For this problem, we will use two different operations.
First, let's find what the left side of the decimal evaluates to. We do this by using the division/remainder method that we learned before. Make sure not to use the decimal part of the number. 

 30 / 2 = 15, R = 0 
 15 / 2 = 7, R = 1 
 7 / 2 = 3, R = 1 
 3 / 2 = 1, R = 1 
 1/2 = 0, R = 1

Now that we're done this, we know the left part of the binary number is 11110
To calculate the right side of the number, we do something a little different. We take the decimal number (in this case, .78125) and then double it.
Depending on if this result is greater or less than 1, we put a 1 or a 0 in our decimal number. 

 .78125 * 2 = 1.5625

Since our result is greater than or equal to 1, we are going to put a 1 in our binary number after the decimal point. Then, we will subtract 1 from the result and repeat the process. 

 11110.1

We repeat this process until we cannot any further. 

 .5625 * 2 = 1.125

Since this number is greater than or equal to 1, we put a 1 in our binary number, then repeat. 

 11110.11

We continue to do this 

 .125 * 2 = .25

Since this result is NOT greater than or equal to 1, we put a zero in the binary number. 

 11110.110

We continue with .25 

 .25 * 2 = .5

This result is not greater than or equal to 1, so we put a 0 in our number. 

 11110.1100

Finally, we do 

 .5 * 2 = 1

Since 1 is greater than or equal to 1, we put a 1 on our number. 

 11110.11001

So, 30.7812510 is 11110.110112 in binary. 

-----------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------> Example 2

Convert 11.12510 to binary.
First, we find out what the left side of the decimal evaluates to 

 11 / 2 = 5, R = 1 
 5 / 2 = 2, R = 1 
 2 / 2 = 1, R = 0 
 1 / 2 = 0, R = 1

So, the left side of our binary number evaluates to 1011
Let's find the portion after the decimal point now. 

 .125 * 2 = .25

Since our result is not greater than or equal to 1, we put a 0 right after the decimal point. 

 1011.0

Next, we repeat this with .25 

 .25 * 2 = .5

Since this result is not greater than or equal to 1, we put another 0. 

 1011.00

Lastly, we repeat with .5 

 .5 * 2 = 1

Since this result is greater than or equal to 1, we put a 1. 

 1011.001

After subtracting 1 from 1, we get 0. So, this was our last iteration.

Thus, 11.12510 is 1011.0012 in binary.
